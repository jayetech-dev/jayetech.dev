Hi, I’m judy henry, though I usually go by jaye in creative spaces.

I’m currently studying Cyber Criminology at Florida State University, a program that blends both computer science and criminology. Because of that, I’ve spent a lot of time working across technical courses as well as studying how technology, security, and people interact in the real world.

Most of my work right now is focused on front-end development and UX/UI design. I like building things that are both useful and visually interesting, and I’m especially drawn to projects where design and development meet. Over time, I’m hoping to grow further into full-stack development so I can build entire systems from the interface down to the logic behind it.

Outside of programming, I’ve always been a creative person. A lot of the visuals on this site — including many of the icons and illustrations — were drawn by hand by me. I like mixing art with technology and experimenting with ways to make websites feel a little more personal and interactive.

You’ll probably notice small pieces of my life scattered throughout the site too, things like art, photography, plants I’m growing, and the occasional appearance from my two dogs.

If you’re visiting on the homepage, the room illustration above is interactive, so feel free to click around and explore.
Or if that’s not your thing, you can just scroll, everything is listed below.






Outside of development, I enjoy creative and hands-on hobbies that have been part of my life for years.
Some of my favorites include: ice skating, sewing, crocheting, painting, digital illustration.
I started crocheting and painting when I was young while spending time at my grandmother’s house. Those early creative hobbies eventually grew into a love for digital art and design, which now influences the way I build and design software.
